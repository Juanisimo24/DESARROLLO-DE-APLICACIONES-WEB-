My 5 favorie Destinations

Copacabana (Río de Janeiro)

Copacabana is one of the most famous neighborhoods in the city of Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Formerly called Sacopenapã, it owes its current name to the visit of the Virgin of Copacabana, whose sanctuary is located in Copacabana, Bolivia, who gave honor to the name to the cultural and religious influence of Bolivia in Brazil. Located in the southern zone of the city, Copacabana has a crescent-shaped beach and is nicknamed the Little Princess of the Sea due to its aura in the 30s, 40s and 50s. A bohemian, glamorous and wealthy neighborhood, Copacabana gave rise to numerous events and artistic movements, becoming a tourist reference. The neighborhood has a large number of restaurants, cinemas, banks, synagogues (normally open to the Jewish community of Rio), commercial establishments and theaters.

  • Province/State: Rio de Janeiro
  • Country: Brazil
  • Population: 160,000
  • Latitude & Longitude: -22.9711, -43.1822

Kyoto, Japan

Kyoto, a city on the island of Honshu, is famous for its numerous classical Buddhist temples, as well as its gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. It is known for its traditional festivals and rich cultural history.

  • Province/State: Kyoto Prefecture
  • Country: Japan
  • Population: 1,460,000
  • Latitude & Longitude: 35.0116, 135.7681

Paris, France

Paris, the capital of France, is a major European city and a global center of art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide avenues and the River Seine. In addition to landmarks such as the Eiffel Tower and Notre Dame Cathedral, the city is known for its café culture and art museums, including the Louvre and the Musée d'Orsay.

  • Province/State: Île-de-France
  • Country: France
  • Population: 2,140,000
  • Latitude & Longitude: 48.8566, 2.3522

New York, USA

New York, a city located in the state of New York, is one of the largest and most well-known cities in the world. It is famous for its skyscrapers, such as the Empire State Building, and for its vibrant cultural life, which includes Broadway, world-class museums, and a diverse food scene.

  • Province/State: New York
  • Country: USA
  • Population: 8,336,000
  • Latitude & Longitude: 40.7128, -74.0060

Sydney, Australia

Sydney, the capital of the state of New South Wales, is the largest city in Australia. It is known for its harbour, the Sydney Opera House and the Sydney Harbour Bridge. The city is also famous for its beaches, national parks and a vibrant cultural scene.

  • Province/State: New South Wales
  • Country: Australia
  • Population: 5,312,000
  • Latitude & Longitude: -33.8688, 151.2093
